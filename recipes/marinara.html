Marinara

Homemade marinara sauce, perfect to accompany any pasta!

This quick recipe is so simple, only using 6 ingredients. A no brainer, for sure

Utilizing some beautiful San Marzano canned tomatoes, this sauce is sure to blow your socks off

Ingredients:

  • Olive Oil - 2 Tbsp
  • Garlic - 8 Cloves
  • White/Yellow Onion - 1 Each
  • San Marzano Tomatoes, Peeled - 28oz Can
  • Salt - To Taste
  • Basil - For Garnish

Steps:

  1. Start by mincing your garlic cloves, 'small' dicing your onion, and hand crushing your tomatoes. Mise en place, as the french call it
  2. Add your olive oil to a medium sized sauce pot, heat over medium low heat, and add your garlic
  3. Stir your garlic often, as it will burn if you do not. Cook until a fragrant golden brown color comes about
  4. Add your onion into the pot. Stir to coat in oil, and season generously with salt to release water and break down the onion
  5. Again, stir often, so no burning occurs. Once translucent and soft, add your hand crushed tomatoes
  6. This is where the magic happens. Let cook slowly for 30 minutes to upwards of an hour. The longer you cook, the more the flavors meld and grow
  7. Season to taste with salt, and it is now your choice to leave slightly chunky or puree for a smoother sauce
  8. Serve with pasta (or whatever else) of choice, garnish with fresh basil if you'd like
  9. Enjoy!
